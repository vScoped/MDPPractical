The Solar System



Definition of a Solar System:
A solar system is a group of large objects, planets and rocks floating in space that surround a sun.



How It Works.

The sun has such a large amount of gravity that pulls everything towards it. The planets are moving extremely fast, so they are getting slingshotted around the sun in what is called an orbit, which is an imaginary circular line around an object in space that other objects follow. At the same time, the sun and planets are moving in space at inhumanly possible speeds, travelling towards nowhere.

Our Solar System

Our Solar System consists of 1 sun, the centre of everything and 8 planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. The Sun pulls the planets together together by a force called Gravity. On top of this, these planets have 146 moons that orbit each planet, Earth only has one moon, however other planets have many moons, such as Jupiter which has 80 moons surrounding it. The distance between a planet and the sun inhibits certain properties for the planet. Planets are mainly affected by the temperature. A large reason for there being life on Earth is that it is a suitable distance from the sun to hold life that wouldn’t be set on fire or frozen instantly. Earths distance from the Sun is 147million kilometres and the average temperature is 14°. Other planets are not as lucky. The distance from the Sun to Mercury is 48million kilometers and average temperature is 178°. That is how hot brownies are in the oven! On the other side Neptune is 4.5 billion kilometres from the sun -225 Celsius. Your freezer gets to -18 degrees, any life would freeze and die instantly.

A Brief Explanation of Gravity.

Everything has gravity, however the amount in everyday objects is so small that it doesn’t have an effect. With massive objects, such as planets, the amount of force which pulls objects towards it is much larger, resulting in a gravitational field which oulls everything on the planet and around it towards it.

If you jump on Earth, you can see that there is gravity because you are pulled down. Gravity is altered on different planets of our solar system. Jupiter’s gravity is 2.4 times more than Earth’s, meaning jumping would be extremely hard. Pluto’s is 1/15th of Earths, jumping would be insanely easy.
