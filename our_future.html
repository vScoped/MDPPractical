Our Future

Humans aren't Developed

As of right now our technology is not developed enough to expand to any planets. We are still in the early stages of understanding and moving into space, there are many years of improving and understanding our technology before anything extremely significant is done for humans in space.



Life off Earth

Not too long away there are plans for people to have lives off Earth and on close planets. The most recent plan is to have a small town on the moon, this would only have scientists but would allow for humans to know more about space and how to expand humanity to live far away. There are also plans to go and stay on Mars, there has proven to be water frozen on Mars that will be used to power the spaceships, a drink for humans, and allow the plants to survive, creating food for both humans and livestock. Water will also allow for humans to create a mini environment that won’t get destroyed by the heat of the sun. By there being water on Mars, there is a possibility that many other planets have water, meaning that humans can travel further and further.

Cities of Space


In 100s of years there is a high probability that there will be space cities. These cities would be floating through space travelling from planet to planet to get resources and have everything needed for humans to live on it forever.

Additionally, there will also be futuristic cities on planets, maybe even taking up the entire planet with just a single city. These humongous cities will eventually be common in galaxies and travel between them could be very fast, people just need to get better at travelling before this is achievable.




Like the Movies?

Many movies that you have seen have shown the different possibilities of humans on planets. For example, a ‘Star Wars’ galaxy would be an ultimate future for humans with planets being cities and humans and aliens living together. However, we must be careful about our future or else humans may become lazy and not care anymore about the environment or other planets like in ‘Wall-E’.
